    "Owning" Your Movements (SFR)

    • Stimulus-to-Fatigue Ratio: Your goal is the highest possible muscle stimulus with the least joint/systemic fatigue.
    • Choose exercises you FEEL perfectly in the target muscle. If an exercise causes pain, find an alternative. A "good" exercise is only good if it's good *for you*.

    The 3 Tiers of Fatigue

    • Tier 1: Muscle Soreness (Good): This is the stimulus. Embrace it.
    • Tier 2: Connective Tissue Stress (Warning): Nagging, achy joints (elbows, knees, shoulders). This is a sign to manage load or swap an exercise.
    • Tier 3: Systemic/CNS Fatigue (Red Alert): Low motivation, can't sleep, strength drops across all lifts. This is a mandatory sign to take a deload.

    Logbook Philosophy

    • Your logbook tracks more than numbers. Use it to note execution.
    • "10 reps, felt every fiber contract" is a bigger WIN than "12 sloppy reps."
    • Progressive overload is law, but not at the expense of form that keeps you healthy.

    J3U Pro-Vault

    Training Philosophy

    Stimulus to Fatigue Ratio (SFR): The core of everything. Your goal is the highest muscle stimulus with the least joint/systemic fatigue. Choose exercises you FEEL perfectly. "Good form" is form that works for YOUR body.
    Logbook is Law: Progressive overload is non-negotiable. If you are not adding weight or reps over time, you are not growing. Period.
    Failure is a Tool: True failure is the last perfect rep you can do. It's a tool to be used strategically on the final sets, not an excuse for sloppy form.

    Recovery & Longevity

    Your Limit is Systemic: Your muscles can recover from almost anything. Your joints, tendons, and Central Nervous System (CNS) cannot.
    Listen to Your Joints: Muscle soreness is the goal. Sharp, achy joint pain is a signal to stop and use an alternative exercise.
    Mandatory Deloads: Every 8-12 weeks, take a deload. Cut sets by 50% and do not train to failure. This is when your connective tissues heal, preventing career-ending injuries.

    The X-Frame Blueprint

    Delts: Prioritize the medial (side) head with various lateral raises. The side delt creates the illusion of width.
    Lats: Focus on exercises that allow a full stretch and drive your elbows down and in.
    Quads: Emphasize the vastus lateralis (the "sweep") with deep, full range of motion.
    Waist: Daily vacuums and contractions are mandatory.
